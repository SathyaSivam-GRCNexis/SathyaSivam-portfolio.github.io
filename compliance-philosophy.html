Compliance Philosophy
A risk-first, evidence-driven approach to governance, risk, and compliance for SaaS platforms
Core Belief

Compliance is not a destination — it is a continuous, risk-driven operating discipline. Frameworks exist to manage risk, not to produce documentation. My approach treats every control as a risk treatment, every audit as a validation exercise, and every gap as a prioritized business decision. The goal is not to be "compliant" — it is to be operationally resilient and audit-ready at any point in time.

Five Governing Principles
1. Risk Before Compliance
Compliance requirements are secondary to risk understanding. I identify business risk first, then map applicable frameworks — never the reverse. This prevents checkbox compliance and ensures controls address real threats.
2. Severity-Based Prioritization
Not every risk demands the same response. Using a 5×5 risk matrix with 6 scoring systems, I ensure Critical and High risks are escalated and treated before Medium or Low findings consume resources.
3. Evidence-Driven, Not Opinion-Driven
Every finding is backed by evidence. I score evidence across 4 dimensions — completeness, freshness, format, and coverage — with quality grades (A–F). If evidence quality is poor, the control is flagged regardless of claims.
4. Governance by Design
Compliance checkpoints are embedded into the SDLC, not applied after release. Security gates at each development phase ensure risks are identified and treated before they become audit findings.

Translate Risk Into Business Language

A risk register that leadership cannot read is a risk register that gets ignored. When presenting to stakeholders, I translate technical risks into business impact — revenue exposure, regulatory penalty ranges, and customer trust metrics. The 5×5 heat map I designed is built to give executives a 30-second view of organizational risk posture, active mitigations, and items requiring escalation.
