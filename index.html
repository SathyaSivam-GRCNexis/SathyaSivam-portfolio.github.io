GRC Mindset

How I Think

The principles that guide how I approach every security review, risk assessment, and audit.

01

Risk Before Compliance

I never start with a checklist. I start with the question: "What can go wrong, and how would it affect the business?" When I assessed risks for a SaaS platform across 5 categories (Engineering, Operational, Vendor, Legal, Business), every security decision was driven by a scored risk — not by which rule came next in a standards document.

02

Severity-Based Prioritization

Not all gaps are equally urgent. When I found 6 types of compliance gaps across 12 frameworks, I classified each by severity — Critical, High, Medium, Low. A missing security safeguard protecting customer data is Critical. A missing documentation link is Low. Resources go where the risk is highest.

03

Governance by Design

Security should be built into how teams work — not added after the fact. When I designed security checkpoints for a SaaS platform's development process, I placed compliance gates at every phase with 5 trigger types. This means security is checked during development, not discovered as a problem after release.

04

Evidence-Driven, Not Opinion-Driven

Every finding I raise is backed by proof. When tracking compliance across 434 controls, I score evidence on 4 dimensions — completeness, freshness, format, and coverage — with quality grades from A to F. If the evidence quality is poor, the control gets flagged regardless of what the responsible person claims. Audits should be objective, not based on assumptions.

05

Translate Risk Into Business Language

A risk list that leadership can't understand is a risk list that gets ignored. When presenting to stakeholders, I translate technical security risks into business terms — potential revenue impact, regulatory fines, and customer trust. The risk heat map I designed isn't just for analysts — it gives executives a quick view of where the organization stands, what's being addressed, and what needs urgent attention.
